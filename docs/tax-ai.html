Can I deduct my home office expenses as a self-employed person in Canada?
AI Tax Assistant
📋 Personal Tax (T1): Yes — as a self-employed person you can deduct home office expenses on Form T2125. Your workspace must be your principal place of business or used exclusively for meeting clients. You can deduct a proportional share of rent/mortgage interest, utilities, internet, and maintenance based on the percentage of your home used for work. The deduction cannot exceed your net self-employment income for the year (no losses).

💡 Tax Tip: Calculate your workspace percentage by dividing the office square footage by total home square footage — keep a floor plan on file in case CRA asks.
You
What's the small business deduction rate for Ontario corporations in 2025?
AI Tax Assistant
🏢 Corporate Tax (T2): Ontario CCPCs (Canadian-Controlled Private Corporations) pay a combined rate of 12.2% on the first $500,000 of active business income — that's 9% federal (after the Small Business Deduction) plus 3.2% Ontario provincial. Above $500,000 the combined general rate is 26.5% (15% federal + 11.5% Ontario).

💡 Tax Tip: If you have multiple associated corporations, the $500,000 SBD limit is shared among them — plan accordingly.
